# Sales Playbook

## Ideal Profiles

- Investors: AI infrastructure, fintech, agent systems, Web3 coordination, applied AI safety, proptech, or market intelligence.
- Customers: funds, research teams, hospitality venues, property managers, security operators, and crypto risk communities with painful workflows.
- Partners: organisations with distribution, domain credibility, capital access, technical compatibility, or pilot access.
- Research sponsors: institutions and ecosystems interested in reinforcement learning safety, agent behavior, and market-agent robustness.

## Outreach Process

1. Research the prospect.
2. Write the reason for targeting.
3. Draft with specific venture or research relevance.
4. Review manually.
5. Send from a configured email system only.
6. Log response and next action.

## Discovery Questions

- What workflow exists today?
- What tools are used?
- What is the primary problem?
- How often does it occur?
- What does it cost in time, risk, money, or trust?
- Who owns the decision?
- What budget or resources exist?
- What timeline matters?
- What success metric would justify action?
- What alternatives are being considered?
- What commitment should happen next?

## Investor Questions

- What thesis areas are most relevant?
- What stage do you prefer?
- What is your typical cheque size?
- What is the decision process?
- What diligence is required?
- What concerns would block conviction?
- What strategic value can you bring beyond capital?
- What follow-up commitment makes sense?

## Follow-Up Cadence

Default suggested days: 2, 5, 10, 21, 45.

Each follow-up should add value through research, product progress, a demo, an insight, a customer result, a tailored question, or an introduction.

## Proposal Process

Use this structure: context, problem, current cost or risk, proposed solution, scope, deliverables, milestones, success metrics, commercial model, assumptions, risks, timeline, next step.

## Weekly Rhythm

- Monday: pipeline review.
- Tuesday: outreach.
- Wednesday: discovery and demos.
- Thursday: proposals and follow-ups.
- Friday: evidence, referrals, metrics, lessons, and next-week priorities.

## Win/Loss Review

Record why the opportunity won or lost, what evidence was created, what objection mattered, which source produced it, and what should change in positioning.
